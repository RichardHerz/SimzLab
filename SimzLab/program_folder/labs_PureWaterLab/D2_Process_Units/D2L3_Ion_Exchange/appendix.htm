Debye-Hückel Equation

            In 1923, Peter Debye and Erich Hückel formulated a theory for predicting the activity coefficients of electrolytic solutions by accounting not only for the concentration but also for the potential energy of the ionic interaction.  Their work has served as the foundation for modern ion exchange research.  The highly non-linear Debye-Hückel equation depends on the composition, molality, density, dielectric constant and temperature of the solution and is generally valid for ionic strengths (equation 21) less than or equal to 0.001 molal  (Zemaitis, 1986).
