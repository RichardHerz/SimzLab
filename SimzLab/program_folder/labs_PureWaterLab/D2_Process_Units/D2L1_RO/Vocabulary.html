Vocabulary

Adsorption ~ The adhesion in an extremely thin layer of molecules (as of gases, solutes, or liquids) to the    surfaces of solid bodies or liquids with which they are in contact

Absorption ~ A process in which one substance permeates another

Antiscalant ~ A chemical species that prevents scale from forming on membrane surfaces

Biodegradation ~ Capable of being broken down, especially into innocuous products by the action of living things (as microorganisms)

Brackish ~ A salt and water mixture

Brine ~ Water saturated with or containing large amounts of a salt, especially sodium chloride

Cake ~ A layer or deposit of compacted matter

Chloramines ~ Any of various compounds containing nitrogen and chlorine.  It is commonly used in low concentrations as a disinfectant in municipal water as an alternative to chlorination.

Colligative Property ~ Factors that determine how the properties of a bulk liquid solution change depending on the concentration of the solute in the bulk solution

Concentration ~ The measure of how much of a given substance there is mixed with another substance

Coupling ~ A pair of forces of equal magnitude acting in parallel but opposite directions

Daltons (Atomic Mass Unit) ~ A unit of mass equal to 1/12 the mass of the most abundant isotope of carbon, carbon 12, which is assigned a mass of 12

Decoupled ~ To separate or detach

Desalination ~ The removal of salt (especially from sea water)

Dissociate ~ A general process in which complexes, molecules, or salts separate or split into smaller molecules, ions, or radicals.  The dissociation of salts by solvation in a solvent like water means the separation of the anions and cations.

Efficiency ~ A value that relates the relative amount of work per energy input

Fouling ~ Clogging or obstructing a surface

Flux ~ The amount of a given quantity that flows through a unit area per unit time

Hydraulic ~ A branch of science and engineering concerned with the mechanical properties of liquids

Ion ~ An atom, group of atoms, or subatomic particle with a net charge

Impermeable ~ Not permitting passage

Insoluble ~ When a solvent will not dissolve in a specific solute

Lumen ~ Hollow center channel in a tubule

Medium ~ An intervening substance through which something else is transmitted

Molecular Weight ~ The mass of one molecule of a given substance

Monohydric ~ A compound formed by the union of one water molecule with some other substance, generally forming a neutral body

Oxidizing Agents ~ A chemical compound that oxidizes another substance, the oxidizing agent becomes reduced in the process, describes the loss of an electron by a molecule, atom or ion

Permeate ~ A substance that is able to pass through a membrane

Polyhydric ~ A compound formed by the union of several water molecules with some other substance, generally forming a neutral body

Reducing ~ Describes the gain of an electron by a molecule, atom or ion

Resin ~ A viscous liquid, typically composed mainly of volatile fluid terpenes

Retentate ~ A substance that is unable to pass through a membrane and is retained on the other side

Solvation ~ The attraction and association of molecules of a solvent with molecules or ions of a solute

Selectively permeable ~ A membrane that allows only certain materials to cross it

Semipermeable ~ A membrane that allows only certain materials to cross it

Soluble ~ A solute that can be dissolved in a given solvent

Solute ~ A substance dissolved in another substance, usually the component of a solution present in the lesser amount

Solvent ~ A substance in which another substance is dissolved, forming a solution

Substrate ~ A structure on which another item can be built

Throughput ~ The rate at which product is formed

Turbidity ~ A cloudiness or haziness of water (or other fluid) caused by individual particles that are too small to be seen without magnification, thus being much like smoke in air
