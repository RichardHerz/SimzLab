Vocabulary

Actual (experimental) capacity ~ The capacity of ion exchange resins as determined by titration, dependent on column conditions such as concentration, PH, and temperature.  Less than total capacity

Alumina ~ Aluminum Oxide, Al2O3

Aluminum silicates ~ Al2SiO5, with differing crystalline structures

Amines ~ Organic compounds containing N as the key atom.  Composed of ammonia structure where one or more H atoms are replaced with R groups

Ammonium carbonate ~ (NH4)2CO3

Amphoteric ~ Capable of acting as either an acid or a base

Analcite ~ Na2O·Al2O3·4SiO3·2H2O

Aniline ~ C6H5NH2

Anion ~ Negatively charged atom or molecule

Aqueous ~ A solution in which the solvent is water

Aromatic ~ Containing rings of atoms

Bed Volume ~ The volume of the resin beads plus the voids between particles

Breakthrough ~ The point when the exchange material is completely saturated and unwanted ions begin appearing in the effluent stream

Cation ~ Positively charged atom or molecule

CEDI ~ Continuous Electrolytic De-Ionization

Chelation ~ The reversible binding of a ligand, the chelant, to a metal ion to form a metal complex known as the chelate

Deionization ~ Replacing ions with H+ and HO-

Divalent ~ An atom or molecule with a +2 or –2 formal charge

Divinylbenzene ~ A benzene ring bonded to two vinyl groups, C10H10

Effluent ~ Outflowing

Electrolytic ~ Made up of a solvent and an ionically dissociated solute, conducts electricity

Electrophilic aromatic substitution ~ A reaction in which a hydrogen attached to an aromatic system is replaced by an electrophile reagent

Ferric chloride ~ FeCl3

Gelatinous ~ Viscous, jellylike

Hardness ~ A measure of polyvalent cations present in an aqueous sample

Hydroxyl ~ An oxygen and a hydrogen atom connected by a covalent bond, -OH

Leucite ~ K2O·Al2O3·4SiO2

Ligands ~ An atom, ion, or molecule that covalently donates or shares electrons with one or more central atoms or ions

Lime ~ CaCO3

Matrix ~ The nonfunctional support structure of a resin

Monomer ~ A small molecule "building block" that can bond to other monomers to form a polymer

Monomeric ~ Consisting of monomers

Monovalent ~ An atom or molecule with a +1 or –1 formal charge

Phenol ~ C6H5OH, an aromatic compound

Phosphonium ~ PH4+

POE ~ Point-of-Entry

Polymer ~ A substance made up of molecules with large molecular mass composed of monomers connected by covalent bonds

Porosity ~ Characteristic openness of the resin’s polymer structure

POU ~ Point-of-Use

Resin ~ A gelatinous or solid polymer made from many smaller molecules

Saline ~ Water containing dissolved salts

Semi-permeable ~ A membrane that allows only certain materials to cross it

Soften ~ Reduce the calcium or magnesium ion concentration

Strong Acid Cation ~ Resins containing highly acidic groups to remove cations

Strong Base Anion ~ Resins containing quaternary amines that operate over large pH scales to remove anions

Styrene ~ C6H5CH=CH2, an aromatic hydrocarbon

Sulfonated coal ~ Coal with a sulfonic acid (SO3H) incorporated onto the coal surface

Sulfonium ~ A positively charged sulfur ion carrying three alkyl groups, S+R3

Tannic acid ~ A weakly acidic commercial form of tannin, a polyphenol

TDS ~ Total Dissolved Solids

Volume capacity ~ Resin capacity in terms of the number of ionic groups per volume of bed, experimentally determined

Weak Acid Cation ~ Resins containing carboxylate groups that operate at neutral or high pH to remove cations

Weak Base Anion ~ Resins containing primary amines that operate at neutral or high pH to remove anions

Weight capacity ~ The intrinsic limit to the number of ions that can be exchanged before the regeneration cycle, determined mathematically
