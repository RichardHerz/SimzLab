Vocabulary  
         
  Activity level ~ The activity level of a activated carbon is usually expressed as the total surface area per unit mass.  This can also be thought as the adsorption capacity of the activated carbon sample.  
  Adsorption ~ the process when a gas or liquid solute accumulates on the surface of a solid or liquid.  
  Anthrax ~ A highly infectious usually fatal animal disease found mostly in cattle and sheep.  Can be transmitted to humans, and is caused by the bacterium, Bacillus anthracis.  
  AOX ~ adsorbable organic halogen  
  Bagasse ~ The dry biomass that is left after the extraction of juice from a the crushed stalk of a sugar cane.  
  Beet-sugar sludge ~ Sugar sludge extracted from beets.  
  BOD ~ biological oxygen demand  
  Carbonaceous ~ characteristic of a material that is rich in carbon content, such as a hydrocarbon.  
  Chlorosis ~ An iron-deficiency characterized by a greenish-yellow discoloration of the skin.  
  COD ~ chemical oxygen demand.  
  Contactor ~ An activated carbon contactor is a container made from steel or concrete that houses a carbon bed in which waste water flows through.  
  Desorption ~ Is the opposite process to adsorption.  Where the adsorbed materials (adsorbates) are removed from the adsorbent surface  
  Egyptian papyrus ~ Papyrus is an ancient form of paper that is made from the papyrus plant.  The ancient Egyptians used this form of paper extensively.  
  Epilepsy ~ A neurological disorder distinguished by frequent impulsive motor, sensory, or psychic malfunction.  Impulsive malfunction may be accompanied by seizures and or unconsciousness  
  Equilibrium ~ A reversible chemical reaction in which the rate of the forward reaction is equal to the rate of the reverse reaction  
  Flue dust ~ Fine particles of metal or alloy emitted with the gases of a smelter or metallurgical furnace.  
  GAC ~ granulated activated carbon  
  Hemoperfusion ~ a treatment technique in which large volumes of the patient's blood are passed over an adsorbent substance to remove toxic substances.  
  Influent ~ In process engineering the influent is the initial flow stream into a given process equipment.  
  Inorganic ~ A inorganic compound is any chemical compound that does not include carbon.  
  J.T. Lowitz ~ German scientist who observed the uses of activated charcoals.  
  Karl Wilhelm Scheele ~ German-Swedish pharmaceutical chemist who discovered the adsorptive capabilities of certain types of charcoal.  
  Lampblack ~ A fine soot that is collected from unburned fuel residues usually composed of carbonaceous material.  
  Lignin ~ A chemical polymer that attaches to cellulose fibers and helps harden the cell wall of plants.  Commonly found in wood.  
  Lignite ~ Commonly know as brown coal, lignite is the lowest form of coal  
  Oil shale ~ fine-grained sedimentary rock containing significant amounts of kerogen, from which technology can extract liquid hydrocarbons.  
  Organic ~ In chemistry organics compounds are those that contain carbon.  The majority of all organic compounds can also contain a few other elements such as hydrogen, oxygen, and nitrogen.  
  Oxidation ~ Describes a substance that loses an electron, usually used in conjunction with reduction, which describes the addition of an electron to a substance.  
  PAC ~ powdered activated carbon  
  Petroleum acid sludge ~ a black, vicous substance left from treating petroleum for impurities using sulfuric acid.  
  Petroleum coke ~ A carbonaceous solid derived from oil refinery coker units or other cracking processes.  
  Physiochemical ~ Relates both to physical and chemical properties.  Also relates to physical chemistry, a branch of chemistry dealing with the physical properties of chemical substances.  
  Potassium ferrocyanide residue ~ K4[Fe(CN)6]·3H2O, lemon-yellow monoclinic crystals.  
  THM ~ trihalomethane  
  TOC ~ total organic carbon  
  TOX ~ total organic halogen  
  Von Ostrejko ~ Inventor who set the foundation for the commercial uses for activated carbons.
