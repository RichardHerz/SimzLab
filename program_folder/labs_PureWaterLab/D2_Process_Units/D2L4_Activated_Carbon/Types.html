Types

 

The performance and application of activated carbon is strongly dependent of the characteristics possessed by the given carbon sample.  The particle size and shape are some of the more important characteristics.  The size affects the rate of adsorption and the size and shape can both affect the pressure drop in an activated carbon bed.  There are two main forms of activated carbon used in industry: granular activated carbon (GAC) and powered activated carbon (PAC).  PAC has a much smaller average mesh size and more uniform shape than GAC.  GAC has more of a random irregular shape.  Table 1 provides a few characteristics that are exhibited by the different kinds of activated carbon.

In deciding which form of activated carbon is right for the job, there are a few things that need to be addressed.  Due to the larger size, GAC costs more per weight than PAC.  The higher cost can be offset, as GAC tends to have better efficiency and PAC is not a good candidate for regeneration due to its size.  This is not always the case but PAC has a higher percentage of carbon loss during regeneration than GAC.  Therefore, PAC should not be used where the rate of exhaustion is fairly high.
